Eggs Recipe

crambled eggs are a quick and easy breakfast dish made by beating eggs with salt, pepper, and a splash of milk.

Ingredients (Per 1-2 Servings)

  • Eggs: 3 large eggs
  • Butter: 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • Liquid: 1 teaspoon to 1 tablespoon of milk, heavy cream, or water (optional—makes them creamier)
  • Seasoning: \(\frac{1}{8}\) teaspoon salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Optional Garnishes: Freshly chopped chives, a sprinkle of cheese, or chili crisp

Steps

  1. Prep the Eggs: Crack the eggs into a mixing bowl. Add your milk (if using) and salt. Whisk vigorously for about 20 to 30 seconds until the yolks and whites are fully combined and slightly frothy.
  2. Melt the Butter: Place a small non-stick skillet over medium-low heat. Add the butter and let it melt completely. Swirl the pan to coat the bottom. When it starts to gently foam (but before it browns), it's ready.
  3. Cook the Eggs: Pour in the egg mixture. Let it sit undisturbed for 5 to 10 seconds until the very edges start to set.
  4. Scramble and Fold: Using a silicone spatula, gently push, pull, or fold the edges of the eggs toward the center of the pan. Continue to fold the eggs slowly, allowing the uncooked liquid to run onto the hot pan, until you have soft, beautiful curds.
  5. Remove from Heat: Turn off the heat just before the eggs look completely done (they should still look slightly wet and glossy). The pan's residual heat will finish cooking them as you serve, preventing them from drying out.
  6. Season and Serve: Transfer to a plate, add black pepper, garnish with chives or toppings, and serve immediately.
