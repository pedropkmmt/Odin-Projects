Lasagna Recipe

Lasagna is a classic Italian baked pasta casserole. It consists of alternating layers of flat, wide pasta sheets, a savory meat or vegetable tomato sauce, and a rich, creamy cheese filling

Ingredients

  • Meat: 1 lb ground meat (a mix of \(\frac{1}{2}\) lb lean ground beef and \(\frac{1}{2}\) lb ground pork or Italian sausage)
  • Aromatics: \(\frac{1}{2}\) cup minced onion and 1-2 cloves minced garlic
  • omato Base: 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes and 1 (8 oz) can tomato sauce
  • Seasoning: 1\(\frac{1}{2}\) tsp dried basil, \(\frac{1}{2}\) tsp dried oregano, 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley, salt, and black pepper (to taste)
  • Ricotta or Cottage Cheese: 1 lb of small-curd cottage cheese or ricotta cheese
  • Hard Cheese: \(\frac{3}{4}\) cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Binder: 3 large eggs
  • seasoning: 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley, salt, and pepper
  • Topping: 1 lb (approx. 4 cups) shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Noodles: 1 (16 oz) package of lasagna noodles (regular or oven-ready)

Steps

  1. Brown the meat: In a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat, brown ground beef and Italian sausage. Drain excess fat.
  2. Sauté aromatics: Add diced onions and garlic, cooking until softened.
  3. Simmer: Stir in tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, and Italian seasonings (basil, oregano). Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low and let it thicken for 20–25 minutes
  4. Mix the cheeses: In a medium bowl, combine ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan, chopped fresh parsley, salt, and black pepper.
  5. Bind: Stir in a beaten egg. This helps the ricotta mixture set and prevents it from oozing when cut.
  6. Bind: Stir in a beaten egg. This helps the ricotta mixture set and prevents it from oozing when cut.
  7. Prepare the pan: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish and spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom (this stops the noodles from sliding).
  8. First layer: Place a single layer of lasagna noodles (boiled al dente or oven-ready) over the sauce. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture, then a layer of meat sauce, and top with shredded mozzarella cheese.
  9. Repeat: Repeat these layers two to three times, ending with a final layer of noodles.
  10. Top it off: Cover the top pasta layer with any remaining meat sauce, and sprinkle generously with more mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses.
  11. Cover and bake: Cover the baking dish tightly with aluminum foil (tip: spray the foil with cooking oil so the cheese doesn't stick). Bake for 40 minutes.
  12. Melt and brown: Remove the foil and bake for another 15–20 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbling and golden-brown.
  13. Rest: Let the lasagna rest at room temperature for 15–30 minutes before slicing. This critical step allows the layers to settle so it holds its shape on the plate.
